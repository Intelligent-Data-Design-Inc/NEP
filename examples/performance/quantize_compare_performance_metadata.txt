title: NetCDF-4 Quantization Algorithm Comparison (zstandard + shuffle)

caption: Results from the quantize.c example program in the NetCDF Expansion Pack. Dataset is a 500x180x360 NC_FLOAT temperature array (~129 MB), stored with 10x45x90 chunks. All three quantization algorithms are shown with zstandard level 1 and shuffle as the fixed lossless filter. BitRound NSB values are remapped to NSD equivalents for a common x-axis. Dotted line shows the lossless-only compression ratio baseline. Higher ratio is better; lower error and write time are better.

alt_text: Three vertically stacked line charts with x-axis of NSD (decimal significant digits) 1 through 6. Each chart has three lines representing BitGroom (solid circles), GranularBitRound (dashed open squares), and BitRound (dash-dot triangles). Top chart: compression ratio decreasing with NSD, all three algorithms close together but GranularBitRound slightly higher; dotted reference line shows lossless-only ratio. Middle chart: write time in seconds, GranularBitRound consistently higher (~1.6s) than BitGroom and BitRound (~0.55s). Bottom chart: maximum absolute error in Kelvin, all three converging to near zero above NSD 3.
