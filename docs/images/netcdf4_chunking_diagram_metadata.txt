title: NetCDF-4 Chunking: Storage Layout, Subset I/O, and Trade-offs
caption: NetCDF-4 supports two storage layouts: contiguous (all values written sequentially) and chunked (data divided into fixed-size tiles). Chunked storage enables efficient hyperslab reads—the middle panel shows how a 4×4 subset of a 6×8 variable touches exactly four 2×2 chunks. The table summarizes how chunk size affects I/O amplification and per-chunk overhead.
alt_text: Three-panel diagram. Top panel: a row of ten numbered cells labeled Contiguous, and two rows of five labeled chunks (C0–C9) labeled Chunked. Middle panel: a 6-by-8 grid partitioned into 2-by-2 blocks; a shaded 4-by-4 region marks the requested subset and dashed outlines show the four chunks read from disk. Bottom panel: a three-column table listing Small, Large, and Aligned chunk sizes with their respective advantages and disadvantages.
